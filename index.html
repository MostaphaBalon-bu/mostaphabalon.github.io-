About

The Data
Behind the Work

I'm a Registered Nurse, AWS Cloud Engineer, and applied data scientist — a combination that is genuinely rare in the life sciences and health policy space. With hands-on clinical experience, 3–5 years engineering production cloud infrastructure, and a specialization in health outcomes and policy analytics, I bring something most data scientists can't: I've been at the bedside, in the field, and inside the AWS console keeping the pipelines that support it all running.

Before focusing on data science, I led multiple public health projects in a supervisory capacity — managing teams, navigating real-world health systems, and translating clinical realities into operational decisions. That experience gives me an instinct for where data models succeed or quietly fail in practice, and how to communicate findings to clinical and policy audiences who need to trust what they're seeing.

As an AWS Cloud Engineer, I've designed and maintained scalable data infrastructure using S3, EC2, Lambda, RDS, Redshift, and Glue — the same stack that powers enterprise health analytics platforms. I manage environments with IAM, CloudWatch, and CloudFormation, which means I don't just build models; I can architect, secure, and deploy the infrastructure they run on.

On the data science side, I've built and tuned classification models on clinical datasets, engineered domain-specific features from health records, designed relational databases joining disparate public health sources, and produced executive-ready visualizations, policy briefs, and stakeholder presentations. I work across the full stack — from raw S3 data and SQL schemas to a Tableau dashboard or a two-page brief for legislators.

That rare trifecta of clinical credentials, cloud engineering depth, and data science training means I can own the entire lifecycle — from secure cloud infrastructure to the model output a health system, pharma team, or regulator will trust and act on.
